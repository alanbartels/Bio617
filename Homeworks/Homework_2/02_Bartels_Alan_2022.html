What do you learn that is different than the boxplot?

With ridgelines, one gets a visual of the frequency distribution of the sea ice extent for a given month. For example, February and August do not look dissimilar to other months in their box plot graph. However, in the ridgeline graph, one can see that the distribution of sea ice extent during February and August appear to be bimodal, something that is not apparent in the box plot graph.

Part 4

Use that Color!

annual_ice_extent <- ggplot(data = ice, 
                            mapping = aes(
                              group = Year,
                              x = Month_Name,
                              y = Extent,
                              color = Year
                            ))

annual_ice_graph <- annual_ice_extent + 
  geom_line() +
  scale_color_gradient(low = "turquoise", high = "black", 
                       limits = c(1978, 2016), 
                       guide = "none") +
  labs(title = "Arctic Sea Ice Size 1978-2016", 
       x = "Month", 
       y = "Sea Ice Extent (10^6*km^2)",
       caption = "Data source: nsidc.org") +
  scale_y_continuous(breaks = 0:20) +
  theme_light()
